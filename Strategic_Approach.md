Information-Set Monte Carlo Tree Search (ISMCTS) is the ideal fit for hidden-information PTCG. In this game, the opponent's hand, deck order, prize cards, and face-down active Pokémon are all concealed. We determinize these unknowns by sampling from a belief model — a weighted distribution over plausible opponent states conditioned on every card we've observed through logs, discards, and public play. For each determinization, we use the engine's native search_begin/search_step API to roll forward a rules-accurate simulation, exploring action sequences with UCB1-tuned bandit selection. At leaf nodes, a data-driven heuristic evaluator scores the resulting board state across six axes: HP differential, prize card lead, energy attachment efficiency, type matchup (weakness/resistance from CardData), bench development depth, and retreat flexibility. We run as many simulations as the per-turn time budget allows (iterative deepening with anytime fallback), then commit the real action with the highest expected win-rate across all sampled determinizations. This approach adapts in real-time — it doesn't follow scripted lines but reasons about what the opponent likely holds and what our best response is across many possible futures.
